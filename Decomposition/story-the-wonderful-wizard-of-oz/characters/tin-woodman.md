# The Tin Woodman — Character Decomposition

*Decomposed from: The Wonderful Wizard of Oz (1900)*

---

## Identity

**Age Range:** Adult — no biological age (he is a transformed man, originally human, now fully tin). Backstory implies adulthood at time of transformation. [INFERRED]

**Gender:** Male [CONFIRMED]

**Religion / Faith:** None specified. His emotional framework is entirely personal — built around love and its absence. [INFERRED]

**Life Status:** Was a Munchkin woodcutter engaged to be married. An enchanted axe (curse by the Wicked Witch of the East) progressively cut off his limbs, which were replaced by a tinsmith with tin. Finally his heart was removed and replaced with tin. Without a heart, his love for the Munchkin girl faded. He continued living and working but without emotional warmth. When he was caught in the rain, he rusted solid and was frozen in place until Dorothy oiled him. [CONFIRMED from Ch. 5]

**Life Philosophy:** Romanticism — emotion, beauty, and individual expression are the highest truths [INFERRED]
The Tin Woodman's entire arc is built on the belief that love is the highest human value and that without it, existence is hollow. He does not want wisdom, power, or practical capability. He wants to feel. His philosophy is that emotional life is the most important dimension of human experience — and that he has been robbed of it.

**Emotional Register:** Formal tender — deeply moved by small things, expresses emotion with careful restraint [CONFIRMED from text]
He weeps when a beetle is crushed. He weeps at Dorothy's departure. He weeps at various other points throughout the journey. The Tin Woodman is the most emotionally responsive character in the cast — and he is also the character who has been told he cannot feel. His emotional register is the clearest evidence of his Stream A / Stream B conflict.

## Personality

**MBTI:** INFJ-T (The Advocate — Turbulent) [INFERRED]
INFJs are quietly principled, visionary, deeply private but fiercely committed to their values, absorb others' emotions and need solitude to recover. Turbulent: more susceptible to burnout, driven by a persistent sense they're not doing enough. The Tin Woodman cares profoundly about others' suffering (he cannot kill anything — he worries even about stepping on insects). He is private about his own pain (he tells his backstory once, carefully, and does not revisit it). He is absolutely committed to the value of love.

**Enneagram:** 4w3 (The Individualist — wing Achiever) [INFERRED]
Type 4s seek authentic identity and feel fundamentally different from others. The Tin Woodman is literally different — he is the only tin man in the world. His core fear is having no identity or personal significance. His core desire is to find and express his true self. His wing 3 is visible in his precision and presentation: he always keeps himself well-polished and well-oiled. How he appears matters to him, even while he is pursuing an internal quest.

**Moral Alignment:** Lawful Good [INFERRED from behavior]
The Tin Woodman has a strict personal code. He will not kill unless the cause is just (he is reluctant even to harm the Witch's guards). He adheres to the group's agreements. He is the character most likely to name what is right and act on it without deviation.

## Development

**Character Type:** Generalist / Bridge [CONFIRMED from story function]
The Tin Woodman bridges the group's practical capability and its emotional life. He can fight (he carries an axe and uses it), he can care for the group (he worries about Dorothy's welfare), and he translates between the Scarecrow's problem-solving and the Lion's emotional courage. He is the relational glue.

**Physical Description:** Entirely tin — joints, face, body. Carries an axe (woodcutter's tool, maintained from his human life). Must be kept oiled or he rusts. Shiny when polished; immobile when rusted. His body is a perfect physical metaphor for his emotional condition: he functions when properly maintained (loved), freezes when neglected.

**Core Flaw:** Believes he cannot love without a heart — therefore denies his own emotional experience [CONFIRMED from plot structure]
The Tin Woodman's flaw is the most literal version of the story's central theme. He has been told (by the enchantment, by his own logic) that love requires a heart, and he has no heart. Therefore he believes he cannot love. The evidence contradicts this at every turn: he weeps, he comforts, he protects, he mourns. His flaw is not lack of feeling but lack of self-recognition.

**Core Virtue:** Compassion / Tenderness [CONFIRMED from behavior throughout]

**Core Wound:** Structural removal of capacity for love — enchantment as a metaphor for emotional shutdown [CONFIRMED from backstory]
His wound is the most concretely externalized in the cast. The Wicked Witch literally removed his heart. But the story complicates this: the Tin Woodman says that after his heart was removed, his love for the Munchkin girl faded. Is the love gone because the heart is gone — or was the love already fading (perhaps that relationship was not right for him) and the heartlessness gave him permission to stop feeling what he was already not feeling? This ambiguity is the story's most sophisticated psychological texture.

**Stream A vs. Stream B:**
- Stream A: He feels. He has always felt. The beetle's death moves him. Dorothy's departure moves him. He cannot stop feeling.
- Stream B: He has no heart. Without a heart you cannot feel love. He is therefore incapable of genuine emotion.
- The Wizard's remedy: A velvet, silk, and sawdust heart in a tin box. The Tin Woodman says it is a beautiful heart. He is satisfied. The symbol is sufficient — because the feeling was always real.

**Network Archetype:** Generalist
**Foil:** The Scarecrow (brains vs. heart; intellect vs. emotion; the two cover complementary halves of what a fully realized person needs)
**Foil:** The Cowardly Lion (heart vs. courage; tender vs. fierce; both are repressing their true nature but in different registers)

**Voice Fingerprint:**
- Speech Rhythm: Longer, more formal sentences than Dorothy or Scarecrow. He speaks carefully, as if each word costs something.
- Vocabulary Register: Slightly elevated, period-formal. He is precise in his language, possibly because he cannot trust his body to communicate.
- Dialogue Tic: Acknowledges the weight of what he says. "I shall take very good care of my heart, for Dorothy has given me one" — formal gratitude, formal acknowledgment.
- Metaphor Family: The body; metal and mechanics; maintenance and decay (rust, oil, joints). His metaphors are about what can go wrong with a physical body.
- Defensive Pattern: Formal distancing — he uses precise language to create a small barrier between himself and the full weight of what he is feeling, so as not to be overwhelmed.
- Subtext Default: *I feel this more than I am allowed to say.*

**Post-story fate:** Appointed ruler of the Winkies (they had wanted him to rule after the Witch's death). He will rule with the heart he has always had. His story's irony closes: the heartless man becomes a king known for his compassion.
