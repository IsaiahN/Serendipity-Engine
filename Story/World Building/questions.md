# World Building Questions

*Questions to construct the world the story inhabits — its rules, its history, its textures, and its contradictions.*

---

## Rules & Society
- What are the explicit rules of this society — laws, codes, customs?
- What are the punishments for breaking them, and who decides?
- What are the unspoken rules — the things everyone knows but no one says aloud?
- Who enforces the rules? Can they be bribed, corrupted, or circumvented?
- What was once forbidden that is now allowed? What was once acceptable that is now punished?
- What rule exists in this world that would be incomprehensible or monstrous to an outsider?

---

## Power & Government
- What kind of government or power structure does this world have?
- Who holds power, and who is excluded from it?
- How is power transferred — inheritance, election, conquest, divine right, merit?
- Who benefits from the current structure? Who suffers, and do they know it?
- Is the system stable, fracturing, or already collapsing when the story begins?
- Who is trying to change the structure? Who is trying to preserve it?

---

## Environment & Ecology
- What does the physical world look like — geography, climate, landscape?
- What is the relationship between people, animals, and the natural world?
- Are there scarce resources that generate conflict?
- What does the natural world threaten in this world? What does it provide?
- Is the environment itself a character — hostile, indifferent, sacred?

---

## Technology & Infrastructure
- What technologies exist in this world?
- What technologies do not exist that we take for granted?
- How do people travel? How fast? How far can they go?
- How do people communicate across distance?
- Who controls access to information, and who is kept in the dark?
- What does technology make possible — or impossible — for the story?

---

## Culture & Belief
- What do people believe about death, the afterlife, or the divine?
- What myths or founding stories does this society tell about itself?
- What art, music, food, or ritual matters in this world?
- What is considered beautiful? Shameful? Sacred? Profane?
- What does this world celebrate, and what does it mourn?
- What does this culture value above all else — and what does that cost the people in it?

---

## History & Time
- What happened before the story begins that everyone is still living inside of?
- What war, disaster, revolution, or golden age shapes the present?
- What do people in this world misremember or mythologize about their own history?
- What was this world like before — and is that past recoverable, or gone?

---

## Story Justification
- Why must this story be told in this world, and not another?
- What does this setting make possible that no other setting could?
- What truth about the real world does this world reveal, distort, or examine?
- What would be lost if you moved this story to a different time or place?
