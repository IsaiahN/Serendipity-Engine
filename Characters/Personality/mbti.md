# MBTI Types

Myers-Briggs types are built from four axes: Introvert/Extrovert, Intuitive/Sensing, Thinking/Feeling, Judging/Perceiving. Each type also has an Assertive (-A) or Turbulent (-T) variant. Assertive types are more confident and stress-resistant; Turbulent types are more self-critical, perfectionistic, and emotionally reactive — which often makes them more motivated to improve.

---

## Analysts

### INTJ — The Architect
Imaginative and strategic. INTJs have a plan for everything and trust their own vision above all else. They are private, self-sufficient, and relentlessly focused on long-term goals. They struggle with emotional expression and can be perceived as cold or arrogant.
- INTJ-A: Self-assured, decisive, rarely second-guesses themselves
- INTJ-T: More perfectionistic and self-critical; driven by the gap between current reality and their ideal

### INTP — The Logician
Innovative and intellectually insatiable. INTPs are most alive when wrestling with complex ideas. They are precise, detached, and deeply skeptical — including of themselves. Can appear absent-minded or indifferent to social norms.
- INTP-A: More comfortable with uncertainty; lets go of unresolved questions more easily
- INTP-T: Anxious about their own blind spots; prone to overthinking and self-doubt

### ENTJ — The Commander
Bold, strategic, and naturally authoritative. ENTJs see inefficiency as an affront and move quickly to fix it. They are decisive, ambitious, and skilled at rallying others — but can steamroll people in the process.
- ENTJ-A: Confident to the point of stubbornness; less open to feedback
- ENTJ-T: More aware of their impact on others; channels self-criticism into higher standards

### ENTP — The Debater
Quick-witted, intellectually provocative, and endlessly curious. ENTPs love arguing ideas — not to win, but to pressure-test them. They resist routine and authority, and can leave projects unfinished once the interesting problem is solved.
- ENTP-A: Thrives on conflict and challenge; less concerned with being liked
- ENTP-T: More sensitive to how their provocations land; more self-conscious about inconsistency

## Diplomats

### INFJ — The Advocate
Quiet, deeply principled, and visionary. INFJs are rare idealists who think in patterns and long-term human consequence. They are intensely private but fiercely committed to their values. They absorb others' emotions and need solitude to recover.
- INFJ-A: More emotionally stable; holds boundaries more naturally
- INFJ-T: More susceptible to burnout; driven by a persistent sense that they're not doing enough

### INFP — The Mediator
Poetic, introspective, and fiercely individualistic. INFPs live in a rich inner world guided by personal values. They are quietly passionate about causes they believe in and deeply empathetic — but can become lost in idealism or crushed by a world that doesn't match their inner vision.
- INFP-A: More resilient; less prone to spiraling on perceived failures
- INFP-T: More emotionally intense; highly motivated by self-improvement but also self-critical

### ENFJ — The Protagonist
Charismatic, empathetic, and mission-driven. ENFJs are natural leaders who lead through inspiration rather than authority. They are gifted at reading people and building community — but can overextend themselves trying to fix everyone around them.
- ENFJ-A: More confident in their vision; less likely to second-guess decisions
- ENFJ-T: More tuned into others' dissatisfaction; pushes harder to meet everyone's needs

### ENFP — The Campaigner
Enthusiastic, creative, and endlessly people-focused. ENFPs light up a room and can make almost anyone feel seen. They are idea-generators who struggle with follow-through. Deeply idealistic about people and relationships.
- ENFP-A: More easygoing; less bothered when things don't pan out
- ENFP-T: More emotionally intense; highly empathetic and prone to taking things personally

## Sentinels

### ISTJ — The Logistician
Responsible, thorough, and deeply reliable. ISTJs follow through on commitments and expect others to do the same. They respect tradition, structure, and proven methods. Slow to trust but fiercely loyal once they do.
- ISTJ-A: Calm and steady; doesn't dwell on mistakes
- ISTJ-T: Holds themselves to exacting standards; more prone to quiet self-criticism

### ISFJ — The Defender
Warm, conscientious, and protective. ISFJs remember details about the people they care about and express love through acts of service. They are selfless to a fault and often struggle to assert their own needs.
- ISFJ-A: More emotionally grounded; better at setting limits on self-sacrifice
- ISFJ-T: More worried about letting others down; pushes through exhaustion to meet obligations

### ESTJ — The Executive
Organized, direct, and driven by results. ESTJs are natural administrators who create order and hold others accountable. They value competence, honesty, and clear hierarchy — and have little patience for excuses.
- ESTJ-A: Decisive and confident; rarely second-guesses their judgments
- ESTJ-T: More self-critical; pushes themselves as hard as they push others

### ESFJ — The Consul
Social, caring, and deeply community-oriented. ESFJs are attuned to social harmony and go out of their way to make others feel welcome. They care deeply about their reputation and approval — and can be hurt by ingratitude.
- ESFJ-A: More secure; less dependent on external validation
- ESFJ-T: More sensitive to social feedback; highly motivated by wanting to do right by others

## Explorers

### ISTP — The Virtuoso
Quiet, practical, and mechanically gifted. ISTPs learn by doing and are exceptional in a crisis. They are emotionally detached, fiercely independent, and deeply curious about how things work — but can seem unreachable in relationships.
- ISTP-A: More easygoing; doesn't overthink risks
- ISTP-T: More self-questioning; applies the same analytical skepticism to themselves

### ISFP — The Adventurer
Gentle, artistic, and fully present. ISFPs are in tune with their senses and live by their personal values, not social expectations. They avoid conflict but are surprisingly stubborn about the things that matter most to them.
- ISFP-A: More emotionally resilient; explores without needing external reassurance
- ISFP-T: More sensitive and self-doubting; deeply affected by perceived rejection

### ESTP — The Entrepreneur
Bold, perceptive, and action-oriented. ESTPs read a room faster than anyone and act on instinct. They are persuasive, risk-tolerant, and energized by challenge — but can be blunt, impatient, and resistant to long-term planning.
- ESTP-A: More confident and aggressive in pursuit of opportunity
- ESTP-T: More self-aware of their impact; occasionally second-guesses bold moves

### ESFP — The Entertainer
Spontaneous, warm, and magnetically energetic. ESFPs are natural performers who love people and experience. They live fully in the present and lift others' moods effortlessly — but can avoid hard conversations and struggle with long-term commitments.
- ESFP-A: More self-assured; less affected by criticism
- ESFP-T: More emotionally open; deeply affected by conflict and disapproval
