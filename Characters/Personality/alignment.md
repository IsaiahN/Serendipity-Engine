# Character Alignment

*A moral and ethical compass for how a character relates to rules, society, and good vs. evil. Originally from D&D; useful shorthand for behavioral defaults under pressure.*

---

> **Author & each character (individually):** Roll 1. The author's alignment shapes which kinds of moral dilemmas their story takes seriously and which it dismisses.
> **Foil:** Protagonist and antagonist should land on different rows (Good vs. Evil is most legible; Good vs. Neutral is subtler). The most dramatically charged pairing is **same column, opposite row** — Lawful Good vs. Lawful Evil share the same devotion to order and use the same methods; Chaotic Good vs. Chaotic Evil share the same rejection of authority. They can *almost* justify each other's choices, which is more disturbing than pure opposition. True Neutral is most useful as a supporting character who forces both sides to reckon with their certainty.

## Good

**Lawful Good** — Believes in justice, order, and doing the right thing through proper channels. Follows the rules because the rules serve people. A knight, a principled judge, a by-the-book hero.

**Neutral Good** — Does what is good without strong attachment to law or chaos. Helps people because it's right, not because a system demands it. Flexible, kind, pragmatic about methods.

**Chaotic Good** — Believes in doing good but rejects authority, systems, and imposed order. Follows their conscience over the law. A rebel with a cause, a vigilante, a trickster who fights for the little guy.

---

## Neutral

**Lawful Neutral** — Follows codes, systems, and order above all else — not because it produces good outcomes, but because structure itself has value. A soldier who follows orders, a judge who applies the letter of the law.

**True Neutral** — Avoids taking sides; seeks balance. May act in self-interest, may genuinely believe extremes are dangerous. A survivalist, a pragmatist, a nature-aligned figure who sees good and evil as human constructs.

**Chaotic Neutral** — Acts entirely on personal freedom and impulse. Not malicious, but unpredictable. Does what they want when they want, indifferent to order or morality. A wildcard, a drifter, a trickster who helps no agenda but their own.

---

## Evil

**Lawful Evil** — Pursues selfish or cruel ends through systems, rules, and hierarchy. Uses law as a weapon. A corrupt bureaucrat, a tyrant with a code, a villain who operates entirely within the letter of the law.

**Neutral Evil** — Self-interest above all, without the structure of Lawful Evil or the chaos of Chaotic Evil. Will cooperate or break rules depending solely on what serves them. A mercenary, a manipulator, a sociopath operating in the middle.

**Chaotic Evil** — Destruction, cruelty, and self-gratification without restraint or code. Hates systems because systems limit them. A nihilistic villain, a sadist, an agent of pure entropy.

---

## Outside the Axis

**Unaligned** — No meaningful moral framework; operates on instinct, survival, or programming. Animals, certain constructs, very young children, or characters whose alignment is deliberately undefined.
