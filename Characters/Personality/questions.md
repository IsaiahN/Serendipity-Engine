# Personality Questions

*Questions to build the psychological interior of a character — how they think, feel, decide, and behave under pressure.*

---

## Typing
- What is their MBTI type? Assertive (-A) or Turbulent (-T)?
- What is their Enneagram type and wing?
- What is their moral alignment?
- Do their type descriptions feel true to them, or are there meaningful contradictions worth keeping?

---

## Thinking & Decision-Making
- How does this character make decisions — logic, emotion, gut instinct, or deference to others?
- How long do they take to decide? Do they agonize or act first and think later?
- What kind of information do they trust — data, experience, intuition, authority, consensus?
- When they're wrong, do they admit it? Quickly or slowly?

---

## Emotional Interior
- What is their emotional baseline — their default mood when nothing is happening?
- What makes them genuinely happy?
- What makes them genuinely afraid?
- What disgusts them?
- What do they find funny?
- What do they cry about when no one is watching?
- Do they express emotions easily or suppress them? Does that serve them or damage them?

---

## Under Pressure
- What does this character do when they are scared?
- What do they do when they are cornered?
- What do they do when they feel betrayed?
- What is their relationship with authority — do they follow, resist, manipulate, or ignore it?
- What is their response to grief?
- Do they lash out, shut down, flee, fight, or people-please when stressed?

---

## Self-Perception
- How does this character see themselves?
- How do others actually see them?
- What is the gap between those two views, and does the story close it?
- What are they most proud of?
- What do they most want to change about themselves — and do they ever try?
- What lie do they tell themselves that the story will eventually disprove?
