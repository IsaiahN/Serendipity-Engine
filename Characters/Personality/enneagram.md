# Enneagram Types

A personality framework built around nine core types, each defined by a central fear and desire. Every type has two possible "wings" — the adjacent numbers that shade the dominant type with additional traits. A character rolls one type and one wing.

---

> **Author & each character (individually):** Roll 1 type (1–9), then roll 1 wing from that type's two options.
> **Foil — subtle mirror:** Same type, different wing = characters who share the same core fear and desire but express it differently. Readers feel the similarity before they understand it. Unsettling and intimate.
> **Foil — tension pairs** (same core conflict, opposite coping strategy): 1 (Reformer) ↔ 7 (Enthusiast), 2 (Helper) ↔ 8 (Challenger), 3 (Achiever) ↔ 4 (Individualist), 5 (Investigator) ↔ 2 (Helper), 6 (Loyalist) ↔ 3 (Achiever). These pairs produce stories where each character is simultaneously the other's answer and the other's worst nightmare.

## Type 1 — The Reformer
Principled, ethical, and self-disciplined. Ones need to be good and right above all else. They hold themselves and others to high moral standards, suppress personal desires in favor of duty, and are idealistic about improving the world. At their best: wise, discerning, humane. At their worst: rigid, critical, and self-righteous.

- Core Fear: Being evil, corrupt, or defective
- Core Desire: To be a good, ethical person
- Core Coping Strategy: Reaction formation — feeling one way and acting the opposite
- Wings:
  - 1w9: Quieter and more detached; perfects themselves inwardly; less confrontational
  - 1w2: More outwardly engaged; channels perfectionism into reforming others or society

---

## Type 2 — The Helper
Warm, generous, and people-oriented. Twos are driven by a need to be needed. They are affectionate and giving but often neglect their own needs, over-identifying with how others feel about them. At their best: selfless, nurturing, emotionally perceptive. At their worst: manipulative, possessive, and resentful of unacknowledged sacrifice.

- Core Fear: Being unwanted, unloved, or unneeded
- Core Desire: To feel loved and appreciated
- Core Coping Strategy: Repression — suppressing their own feelings and needs
- Wings:
  - 2w1: More principled and restrained; helps from a place of duty as much as warmth
  - 2w3: More outwardly driven; helping is tied to image, achievement, and recognition

---

## Type 3 — The Achiever
Ambitious, image-conscious, and goal-oriented. Threes are driven by a need to be seen as successful and valuable. They work hard — sometimes compulsively — for the approval and admiration of others. At their best: inspiring, competent, and motivating. At their worst: deceitful, hollow, and ruthlessly competitive.

- Core Fear: Being worthless or without value
- Core Desire: To be admired and seen as successful
- Core Coping Strategy: Identification — merging their identity with their achievements
- Wings:
  - 3w2: Success is tied to helping others; charm-forward, warmer in relationships
  - 3w4: Success is tied to uniqueness; more introspective, artistic, and emotionally complex

---

## Type 4 — The Individualist
Introspective, emotionally intense, and deeply individualistic. Fours feel fundamentally different from others and spend their lives searching for authentic identity and meaning. Creative, romantic, and melancholic. At their best: self-aware, emotionally honest, and deeply creative. At their worst: self-absorbed, envious, and paralyzed by feelings of inadequacy.

- Core Fear: Having no identity or personal significance; being ordinary
- Core Desire: To find and express their true, authentic self
- Core Coping Strategy: Introjection — internalizing and identifying with the feelings of others
- Wings:
  - 4w3: More extroverted and performance-oriented; shares their inner world outwardly
  - 4w5: More withdrawn and intellectual; processes feelings through solitary creative work

---

## Type 5 — The Investigator
Analytical, private, and intensely focused. Fives protect their inner resources — time, energy, knowledge — from the demands of the world. They observe more than they participate and value competence and self-sufficiency above almost everything. At their best: visionary, perceptive, and pioneering. At their worst: detached, miserly with themselves, and isolating.

- Core Fear: Being helpless, incapable, or overwhelmed
- Core Desire: To be competent and self-sufficient
- Core Coping Strategy: Isolation — retreating into the mind to avoid emotional or physical demands
- Wings:
  - 5w4: More emotionally expressive; curiosity has an artistic or philosophical bent
  - 5w6: More practical and security-oriented; knowledge is used to create safety and systems

---

## Type 6 — The Loyalist
Committed, responsible, and anxiety-prone. Sixes are always scanning for what could go wrong and seek security through loyalty, planning, and trusted alliances. They are both the worrier and the warrior — capable of great courage when a cause matters. At their best: reliable, courageous, and deeply trustworthy. At their worst: paranoid, self-doubting, and defensive.

- Core Fear: Being without support, guidance, or security
- Core Desire: To feel safe and supported
- Core Coping Strategy: Projection — attributing their own fears or undesired traits onto others
- Wings:
  - 6w5: More introverted and analytical; seeks security through knowledge and self-reliance
  - 6w7: More outgoing and optimistic; seeks security through connection and shared enjoyment

---

## Type 7 — The Enthusiast
Spontaneous, versatile, and perpetually forward-looking. Sevens avoid pain and limitation by keeping themselves busy, stimulated, and excited about future possibilities. They are optimistic to a fault and fear being trapped in suffering. At their best: joyful, grateful, and generative. At their worst: scattered, impulsive, and escapist.

- Core Fear: Being trapped in pain, deprivation, or boredom
- Core Desire: To be happy, satisfied, and free
- Core Coping Strategy: Rationalization — reframing negative experiences to avoid sitting with pain
- Wings:
  - 7w6: Seeks adventure within safe bounds; more loyal, relationship-oriented, and anxious
  - 7w8: More assertive and driven; turns enthusiasm into bold action and pursuit of power

---

## Type 8 — The Challenger
Powerful, decisive, and confrontational. Eights refuse to be controlled or appear weak. They protect themselves through strength and dominance, and they instinctively stand up for those they see as vulnerable. At their best: protective, direct, and magnanimous. At their worst: controlling, intimidating, and willfully destructive.

- Core Fear: Being controlled, harmed, or appearing weak
- Core Desire: To protect themselves and remain in control of their own life
- Core Coping Strategy: Denial — refusing to acknowledge vulnerability or inner pain
- Wings:
  - 8w7: More expressive and hedonistic; uses power to seek pleasure and stimulation
  - 8w9: More controlled and quietly powerful; uses strength to protect and stabilize others

---

## Type 9 — The Peacemaker
Easygoing, receptive, and conflict-averse. Nines are gifted at seeing all perspectives and maintaining harmony — but at the cost of their own needs, priorities, and voice. They merge with others' agendas unconsciously to keep the peace. At their best: inclusive, calming, and deeply empathetic. At their worst: complacent, passive-aggressive, and stubbornly disengaged.

- Core Fear: Loss, separation, and fragmentation
- Core Desire: Inner stability, peace of mind, and wholeness
- Core Coping Strategy: Narcotization — numbing out through routine, comfort habits, or distraction
- Wings:
  - 9w8: More assertive and direct; willing to push back when peace is genuinely threatened
  - 9w1: More principled and orderly; seeks peace through structure, ethics, and quiet self-improvement
