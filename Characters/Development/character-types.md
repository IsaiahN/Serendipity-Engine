# Character Types

> **Each character:** Roll within the role assigned during Cast Architecture. Protagonists roll from the Protagonists section; antagonists from the Antagonists section; supporting characters roll from the Supporting section.

> **Who can be the main character (POV anchor):** Any type in the Protagonists section, including Villain Protagonist. Antagonists can also be the story's POV character \u2014 if the antagonist is your main character, roll them from the Protagonists section instead. The distinction is **point of view, not moral alignment**.

> **Who cannot be the main character:** Any type in the Supporting section. Supporting characters have their own arcs and stakes, but the story does not live inside their head.

> **Deliberate blur:** A villain protagonist + a redeemable antagonist is a valid and interesting configuration \u2014 roll both from their respective sections, then note the inversion. The type is the starting position; the story is what happens when the character is pressured against it.

## Society — The Structural Character

*Before rolling any individual character, define Society. Society is a character. It predates every named character in the story and outlasts most of them. Every character in the story — except the Author — exists inside it and must negotiate with it. They comply, perform compliance, resist, or are destroyed by it.*

*Society is not automatically the antagonist — but it is always a pressure. When it is the antagonist, it is usually carrying one or more Antilife Seals. When it is not the antagonist, it is still the water all the fish swim in.*

*Define Society in Phase 3 and record in `world/world-building.md`. Carry that definition into every character roll: what does this character's age, gender, faith, wound, and life status cost them in this specific Society?*

**Society's character sheet (record in `world/world-building.md`):**
- **Role:** What does Society claim to protect? What does it actually protect?
- **Want:** What does it need to preserve itself? (conformity, hierarchy, purity, productivity, silence)
- **Wound:** Its internal contradiction — the thing it cannot acknowledge about itself
- **Flaw:** The social pathology it perpetuates (scapegoating, erasure, exclusion, exploitation, enforced forgetting)
- **Cost:** What it demands from the characters who live inside it
- **Enforcement:** Who administers the rules — and what happens to those who break them?

## Extras, NPCs & Minor Named Characters

### Minor Named Character
Significant enough to be named and to reappear, or to carry one substantial scene. **Roll:** Name (full) + Age Range + Emotional Register + one notable physical trait. No full character file needed. If they become load-bearing mid-story, promote them and run the full per-character roll suite.

### Extra / Background Character
Scene-present but not narratively significant \u2014 the person at the counter, the face in the crowd, the driver who appears once. **Roll:** Name (optional) + one physical or behavioral detail that makes them visible without being developed. Do not roll psychology, wound, or arc. If they accumulate narrative weight during drafting, promote them to Minor Named.
