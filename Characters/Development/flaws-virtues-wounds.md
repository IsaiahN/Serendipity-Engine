# Character Flaws, Virtues & Wounds

---

> **Author & each character (individually):** Roll 1 flaw + 1 virtue + 1 wound. Constraint: the three must be psychologically coherent — the wound should be a plausible origin for the flaw, and the virtue should exist in genuine tension with the flaw (not simply its opposite). If the combination lacks internal logic, re-roll one element. The incoherence is a signal, not a feature.
> **Foil:** Two characters who share the same wound but developed different flaws in response are natural foils — same damage, different scar tissue. This works especially well for protagonist/antagonist pairs, or for a parent and the child shaped by the same family wound.

## Wounds
*A wound is a formative emotional injury — real or perceived — that shapes the character's deepest fears and core behaviors. It is NOT the same as a flaw; it is the WHY behind the flaw.*

### Childhood & Family Wounds
Abandoned by a parent (physically or emotionally)
Adopted and felt "chosen but replaceable"
Born into poverty and felt shame for it
Conditional love — only loved when performing / achieving
Death of a parent or sibling at a young age
Emotional neglect (needs ignored, not abused)
Feeling invisible in a large family
Grew up as the "parentified child" (cared for parent or siblings)
Grew up in chaos / instability (addiction in home, domestic violence)
Had something important taken or destroyed by a parent
Lost childhood to illness (self or family member)
Never felt good enough for a parent
One parent left / walked out
Parent's favoritism of a sibling
Raised by a narcissistic parent
Raised without affection or physical touch
Religious trauma / strict upbringing that caused shame
Survivor's guilt (sibling or parent died, they lived)
Trauma bonded to a parent — loved and feared the same person
Witnessed violence between parents

### Social & Identity Wounds
Bullied for appearance, disability, or difference
Excluded by a group they desperately wanted to belong to
First experience of racism, sexism, homophobia, or discrimination
Identity erased or invalidated (sexuality, gender, culture, religion)
Moved frequently — never had stable friendships
Never felt like they belonged anywhere
Ostracized or scapegoated by community
Public humiliation that never left them
Social rejection during formative years

### Love & Relationship Wounds
Betrayed by a best friend
Betrayed by a romantic partner (cheating, lying, leaving)
Fell in love and was rejected or ghosted
Given up on / left when they were most vulnerable
Loved someone who was abusive
Loved someone they could never have
Lost a child (miscarriage, death, estrangement)
Loved deeply, then experienced sudden loss (death)
Partner took children in a custody battle
Relationship that slowly erased their identity
Romantic relationship that ended in humiliation
Was someone's "practice relationship" — not taken seriously

### Achievement & Failure Wounds
Dream failed publicly (career, audition, business)
Gave up a dream for someone else's sake
Had success stolen or uncredited
Natural talent dismissed or mocked early on
Never given the opportunity to prove themselves
Plateau reached — never fulfilled potential
Raised to believe they were destined for greatness, then fell short
Sacrificed career for family and resents it (or doesn't, but wonders)
