# Character Development Questions

*Questions about a character's arc — where they start, what the story does to them, and who they become (or fail to become).*

---

## Backstory
- What is the defining event of their past that the reader will eventually learn?
- What wound shaped their core belief about the world?
- What did they lose that they've never recovered from?
- What did they survive that made them who they are?
- What was their life like before the story begins — what was normal for them?

---

## Want vs. Need
- What does this character want? (The external, conscious goal they are actively pursuing)
- What do this character need? (The internal truth they are resisting or unaware of)
- How far apart are those two things at the start of the story?
- Does getting what they want prevent them from getting what they need?

---

## Flaw & Wound
- What is their central flaw?
- Where does it come from — what wound created it?
- How does the flaw manifest as a behavior the reader will observe?
- Who else in the story suffers because of this flaw?
- Is the flaw ever justified, or does it only feel justified to the character?

---

## Arc
- Where does this character start emotionally and morally?
- Where do they end?
- What is the moment of greatest resistance — where they almost don't change?
- What breaks through that resistance?
- If they don't change — what does their refusal cost them, and what does it say?
- Is this a positive arc (growth), negative arc (fall), or flat arc (they stay the same and change the world around them)?

---

## Role in the Story
- What is their function in the plot?
- What do they represent thematically?
- What does the protagonist learn from them — directly or indirectly?
- Could they be cut, or are they load-bearing?
- What would the story lose if they disappeared?

---

## Core Values & Personal Code
- What does this character believe matters most — what they would defend out loud if asked?
- What are the 2–3 rules they actually live by, consciously or not?
- Where did those rules come from — were they chosen, or inherited from survival?
- Is any part of their personal code still serving them, or are they following a rule that made sense once and no longer does?
- What would it take to make them violate their own code — and have they done it before?
- Does their stated value and their actual behavior match? Where is the gap?
- What principle, if violated by someone else, makes them genuinely unable to forgive?

---

## Self-Care & Restoration
- What does this character do when they are depleted, overwhelmed, or in grief?
- Does their restoration mechanism actually reach the wound — or does it only quiet the surface?
- Is there a substance, behavior, or pattern they return to that they know is not helping them?
- What do they need from other people when they are struggling — and do they know how to ask for it?
- What does "feeling okay again" look like for this character — what is the signal that they have recovered?
- What do they believe they deserve when they are suffering?
- Has anyone in their life ever restored them in a way they have not been able to replicate since?
